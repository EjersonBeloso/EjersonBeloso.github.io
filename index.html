Donsol Sorsogon

Luzon

South

Whale Shark

We're starting out big, and by this, we mean, quite literally! There’s nothing quite like the experience of swimming with these peaceful sea giants, and that’s why we think this should not be missing from our list. That said, we want to emphasize our preference for the small village Donsol (Sorsogon, Bicol region) and not for Oslob (Cebu). In Donsol the program was developed together with WWF (World Wide Fund for Nature) Philippines and is more regimented - here the animals are not fed, which changes their natural behavior.

El Nido Palawan

Visayas

West/p>

Coron

This is one of the main reasons why Palawan is repeatedly voted one of the most beautiful islands in the world: El Nido. Although Palawan is also a province in itself, to which quite a few islands belong, El Nido and the Bacuit Archipelago are especially in the spotlight here - so much, so that there is justifiable talk of over-tourism, and even closures are necessary at times. But if you know the right corners, you will have a great time here! Here you will be spoilt with pristine beaches, rich marine life, lagoons, caves, and stunning landscapes and cliff formations.

Mt Hibok Hibok

Visayas

South

Camiguin

There are several elevations on the Philippines islands, the highest mountains are Mount Apo in Davao, Mount Pulag in Luzon, or Dulang Dulang in the province of Bukidnon. Mount "Hibok-Hibok", one of the many volcanoes found on the island of Camiguin, is exemplary for the many great hiking possibilities.

Siargao

Mindanao

South

Siargao Island

Siargao boasts unspoiled white sandy beaches, lush coconut groves, and stunning rock formations. But the most important attraction is probably the internationally known surfing spot, "Cloud 9", best visited during the months of September to November.

Libjo Island

Dinagat Islands

South

Kisses Islets

If you have been to El Nido or want to visit the counterpart in the east, you should take a look at Libjo, Dinagat Islands. Here you can find the hidden gems of Kisses Islets, which are at least as beautiful, but at least twice as difficult to reach. Besides the jaw-dropping limestone, the crystal-clear turquoise waters protected by the striking little islets are calm and beguiling. Dinagat in itself is also well-known for its beaches, resorts, and caves.

South

Carbon Market

One of the oldest urban markets in the country, it surprises us again and again that many travelers avoid the Carbon market or are even afraid to visit it. This is a local affair and a very nice opportunity to experience Philippine everyday life up close and personal. Also, other famous sights like Magellan's Cross or Basilica Sto. Nino is only a few minutes walk away.

Bislig,

Surigao del Sur

South

Carbon Market

Rather known to adventurous trip insiders who also travel to the main island of Mindanao: the Tinuy-An Falls in Bislig, Surigao del Sur. It’s a three-tiered waterfall with natural pools where one can leisurely take a plunge. Many refer to the stunning falls as the Little Niagara Falls of the Philippines. While not as big, one can’t help but be amazed at the sight of Tinuy-an Falls’ curtain of clear waters cascading down its base. On Mindanao, there are many other waterfalls like the Tinago Falls, the Aliwagwag Falls, and the Awao Falls.

Monad Shoal

Malapascua Island.

South

Carbon Market

The only place in the world where thresher sharks can be found almost all year round is Monad Shoal around Malapascua Island. This island is located in the northwest of Cebu and is a must for divers. However, more and more individual travelers find their way here, because Kalanggaman is also not far away and always worth a trip.
